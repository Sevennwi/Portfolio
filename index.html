About

Since the beginning of my studies, programming has been part of my life with scientific data analysis. I became interested in web development when I looked at several laboratory websites during my academic years. Indeed I wanted to be able to create beautiful websites while making science more accessible. So after my biology degree, I carried out multiple online trainings with expert guidance as well as hands-on projects in web design and web development. I now work as a freelance developer, mostly on front-end.

On the side, I am an avid podcast listener as I like to stay up to date on science and programming. I love to learn new tools and I am always excited to learn about new projects.
